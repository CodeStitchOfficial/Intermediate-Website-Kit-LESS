About Laura

An Artistic Journey

Laura Winslow's voyage into the world of art began in her early years. Her first teacher was her mother, Ruth C. Winterbottom, who introduced her to the world of pastels and watercolors. Her artistic exploration continued into her late teens, as she had the privilege of mastering watercolors under the tutelage of the inspiring Edgar Whitney on Long Island.

She broadened her horizon further by gaining a degree in advertising art from the state-renowned SUNY in Farmingdale, New York. Not just content with that, she put theory into practice by playing a pivotal role as a staff artist for the prominent advertising firm, J. Walter Thompson in bustling NYC. To bolster her skillset, she ambitiously attended additional classes at the revered Art Students League of New York, where she fine-tuned her art. During this inspiring period of her life, her works attracted the attention of art enthusiasts and many of her captivating illustrations were bought from Hemri Bendel's.

Venturing outside the city, Laura expanded her artistic domain by working in advertising agencies nestled in the scenic Mid-Hudson Valley of New York, and for a local newspaper in cozy Burlington, Vermont. The move to Vermont offered her a golden opportunity to immerse herself in landscape painting, studying with the remarkable Frank Mason of the Art Students League. Indeed, Laura's artistic journey is a tale of continuous learning, exploration, and dedication to her passion, proving that great art is a culmination of persistence and skill.

Laura Winslow's artistic achievements are complemented beautifully by her closely-knit family life. She has created six children's books, each brimming with her creative illustrations, a gift to her three daughters and nine grandchildren, who have been both her inspiration and her biggest fans. Through life's simple moments and family bonds, Laura finds the balance and motivation to keep her art, just like her family ties, strong and flourishing.
